Circular No: MOF/PROC/2026/DEMO-027
Date: 10 January 2026

Subject: Guidelines on Procurement and Tendering Procedures for Government Departments

1. Purpose

This circular consolidates the procurement thresholds, approval authority levels, and documentation requirements applicable to all government departments, statutory boards, and provincial councils. This document is a synthetic demonstration record prepared for illustrative purposes and does not represent an actual government issuance.

2. Procurement Thresholds

2.1 Purchases up to LKR 100,000 (Demo Currency Units) may be made through direct purchase from a registered supplier, subject to at least three informal price quotations being obtained and recorded.
2.2 Purchases between LKR 100,001 and LKR 2,000,000 shall be subject to a minor tender procedure, requiring a minimum of five sealed quotations advertised departmentally.
2.3 Purchases exceeding LKR 2,000,000 shall be subject to open competitive bidding through the National Procurement Portal (Demo) and shall be advertised for a minimum of 21 calendar days.
2.4 Emergency procurements necessitated by natural disaster, public health emergency, or critical infrastructure failure may bypass standard thresholds subject to post-facto ratification by the Ministry within 14 working days.

3. Approval Authority

3.1 Divisional Heads may approve direct purchases within their delegated financial limits, not exceeding LKR 100,000 per transaction.
3.2 Heads of Department may approve minor tenders up to LKR 2,000,000, subject to certification by the departmental Procurement Committee.
3.3 Open competitive bids exceeding LKR 2,000,000 require approval of the relevant Ministry Procurement Committee and, where the value exceeds LKR 50,000,000, concurrence of the Cabinet Appointed Procurement Committee (Demo).

4. Required Documentation

4.1 A Procurement Requisition Form (PRF-01) signed by the requesting officer and endorsed by the Divisional Head.
4.2 A Comparative Statement of Quotations for all minor tenders and open bids.
4.3 A Board of Survey report where the procurement involves replacement of existing assets.
4.4 A signed Conflict of Interest Declaration from all members of any evaluation committee.

5. Timelines

5.1 Minor tender evaluations must be completed within 10 working days of the closing date.
5.2 Open competitive bid evaluations must be completed within 30 working days of the closing date, extendable once by written justification to the Ministry.
5.3 Payment to the successful bidder shall be processed within 21 working days of delivery acceptance and invoice submission.
