Subject: Public Holidays for the Year 2026 — Government Departments and Institutions

1. Purpose

This circular is issued for the information of all Heads of Departments, Provincial Councils, and Government Corporations, listing the public holidays applicable to government offices for the calendar year 2026. This document is a synthetic demonstration record and does not represent an actual government issuance.

2. General Instructions

2.1 Unless otherwise specified, all government offices, departments, and corporations shall remain closed on the dates listed below.
2.2 Where a holiday falls on a weekend, no additional day off will ordinarily be granted unless a separate circular is issued.
2.3 Essential services (health, security, utilities) shall maintain a duty roster to ensure continuity of service during all holidays.
2.4 Mercantile holidays are marked separately and apply only to institutions governed under the Shop and Office Employees Act.

3. Schedule of Public Holidays — 2026

3.1 4 January — National Day of Reflection (Government holiday)
3.2 14 February — Founders' Day (Government holiday)
3.3 4 March — Agricultural Workers' Day (Mercantile holiday)
3.4 13-14 April — New Year Festival (Government holiday, two days)
3.5 1 May — International Workers' Day (Government holiday)
3.6 22 May — National Heroes' Commemoration (Government holiday)
3.7 20 June — Public Service Day (Government holiday)
3.8 4 August — Independence Commemoration Day (Government holiday)
3.9 15 October — Harvest Festival (Mercantile holiday)
3.10 25 December — Winter Festival (Government holiday)

4. Leave Adjustments

4.1 Heads of Departments may, at their discretion and subject to operational requirements, permit staff to combine annual leave with adjoining public holidays.
4.2 Requests for such combined leave must be submitted at least seven working days in advance through the normal leave application procedure described in the HR Circular on Leave Entitlements.

5. Public Notification

5.1 All departments dealing directly with the public shall display this holiday schedule prominently at public-facing counters and on departmental notice boards no later than 15 January 2026.
5.2 Any urgent matter arising during a holiday period should be directed to the duty officer nominated by the respective Head of Department.
