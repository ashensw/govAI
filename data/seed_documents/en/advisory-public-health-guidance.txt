MINISTRY OF HEALTH (DEMO)
Advisory No: MOH/PHA/2026/DEMO-041
Date: 8 January 2026

Subject: Seasonal Dengue Prevention Advisory

1. Purpose

This advisory is issued to all regional health offices, government departments, schools, and the general public regarding preventive measures against dengue fever during the current high-risk season. This document is a synthetic demonstration record and does not represent an actual government issuance.

2. Situation Overview

2.1 A seasonal increase in mosquito-borne illness is anticipated between January and April due to expected rainfall patterns.
2.2 Regional health offices are directed to intensify surveillance and community engagement during this period.

3. Symptoms to Watch For

3.1 Sudden high fever lasting more than two days.
3.2 Severe headache, pain behind the eyes, and joint or muscle pain.
3.3 Skin rash appearing two to five days after onset of fever.
3.4 Mild bleeding such as nosebleeds or bleeding gums, which requires immediate medical attention.

4. Prevention Measures for the Public

4.1 Eliminate stagnant water in and around the home, including flowerpot trays, discarded containers, and roof gutters, at least once a week.
4.2 Ensure water storage containers are covered at all times.
4.3 Use mosquito nets, repellents, and protective clothing during peak mosquito activity hours (dawn and dusk).
4.4 Report suspected clusters of fever in a neighbourhood to the nearest Public Health Inspector's office.

5. Duties of Health Officers

5.1 All Public Health Inspectors and Medical Officers of Health must submit weekly dengue case reports to the Regional Director of Health Services using Form MOH-D7.
5.2 Fogging operations shall be conducted every Tuesday in zones identified as high-risk based on the previous week's case data.
5.3 Larval surveys shall be conducted fortnightly in all Grama Niladhari divisions with a Breteau Index above the regional threshold.
5.4 Public awareness sessions shall be conducted in schools and community centres at least once per month during the high-risk season.

6. Reporting and Escalation

6.1 Any cluster of five or more suspected cases within a one-kilometre radius within a seven-day period must be reported to the Regional Epidemiology Unit within 24 hours.
6.2 Hospitals shall notify all confirmed dengue admissions to the Epidemiology Unit (Demo) within 24 hours of diagnosis.

7. Public Enquiries

Members of the public may contact the Health Information Hotline at 1999 (Demo Hotline) or email health.advisory@demo.gov (Demo Address) for further guidance.
