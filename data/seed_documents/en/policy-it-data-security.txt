DEPARTMENT OF INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY (DEMO)
Policy No: DIT/SEC/2026/DEMO-003
Date: 12 January 2026

Subject: Internal IT and Data Security Policy for Government Employees

1. Purpose

This policy establishes the minimum information security standards applicable to all government employees who access departmental systems, networks, or data. This document is a synthetic demonstration record and does not represent an actual government issuance.

2. Data Classification

2.1 All departmental information shall be classified as Public, Restricted, or Confidential.
2.2 Public information may be shared without restriction.
2.3 Restricted information is limited to the originating department and may only be shared externally with the written approval of the Head of Department.
2.4 Confidential information shall be accessible only to specifically authorised officers and shall never be transmitted over unencrypted channels or stored on personal devices.

3. Password and Account Requirements

3.1 All system passwords must be a minimum of 12 characters, combining upper and lower case letters, numbers, and symbols.
3.2 Passwords must be changed at least once every 90 days and must not repeat any of the previous 5 passwords used.
3.3 Multi-factor authentication is mandatory for access to any system handling Restricted or Confidential data.
3.4 Shared or generic accounts are prohibited except for designated public information kiosks approved by the Department of Information Technology (Demo).

4. Handling of Classified Data

4.1 Confidential data must be encrypted both at rest and in transit using departmentally approved encryption standards.
4.2 Physical documents containing Confidential data must be stored in locked cabinets and logged in the departmental register when removed or returned.
4.3 Confidential data must never be forwarded to personal email accounts or third-party cloud storage services not approved by the Department of Information Technology (Demo).
4.4 Any device used to access Restricted or Confidential systems must have departmentally issued endpoint protection software installed and kept up to date.

5. Acceptable Use of Email and Internet

5.1 Departmental email accounts shall be used for official business only; incidental personal use is permitted provided it does not interfere with duties or system performance.
5.2 Employees must not open attachments or links from unverified senders and must report suspected phishing attempts to the IT Security Desk immediately.
5.3 Access to social media and streaming services on departmental networks is restricted to designated break periods, where technically enforced.

6. Incident Reporting

6.1 Any suspected data breach, lost device, or unauthorised access must be reported to the IT Security Desk within 2 hours of discovery, using Form DIT-INC-01.
6.2 The IT Security Desk shall convene an incident review within 24 hours of any confirmed breach involving Confidential data and notify the Department Head accordingly.
6.3 Failure to report a known security incident may result in disciplinary action under the Public Service Conduct Regulations (Demo).

7. Enquiries

Enquiries regarding this policy may be directed to the IT Security Desk, Department of Information Technology (Demo), reference DIT/SEC/2026/DEMO-003.
