DEPARTMENT OF PUBLIC ADMINISTRATION (DEMO)
Circular No: DPA/HR/2026/DEMO-009
Date: 5 January 2026

Subject: Leave Entitlements and Application Procedure for Government Employees

1. Purpose

This circular sets out the categories of leave available to permanent government employees, the entitlement periods, and the procedure for applying. This document is a synthetic demonstration record and does not represent an actual government issuance.

2. Categories and Entitlements

2.1 Annual Leave: Confirmed employees are entitled to 24 working days of annual leave per calendar year, credited in full at the start of the year.
2.2 Casual Leave: 7 days per calendar year, intended for short-notice personal matters, not carried forward.
2.3 Medical Leave: 20 days per calendar year on production of a medical certificate for absences exceeding two consecutive days; the first two days of any illness may be self-certified up to three times per year.
2.4 Maternity Leave: 84 calendar days for the first two live births, and 42 calendar days for subsequent births, on full pay.
2.5 Paternity Leave: 3 working days, to be availed within 30 days of the birth.
2.6 Duty Leave: Granted for official training, examinations, or assignments, subject to prior written approval of the Head of Department.

3. Carry-Forward Rules

3.1 A maximum of 12 days of unused annual leave may be carried forward to the following calendar year; any balance beyond this shall lapse on 31 December.
3.2 Casual leave and medical leave entitlements do not carry forward under any circumstance.
3.3 Carried-forward annual leave must be utilised within the first six months of the following year, failing which it shall lapse.

4. Application Procedure

4.1 Leave of any category must be applied for using Form HR-04, submitted to the immediate supervisor for endorsement and thereafter to the Head of Department for approval.
4.2 Annual leave exceeding 5 consecutive working days requires at least 14 days advance notice, except in cases of genuine emergency.
4.3 Medical leave exceeding 2 days must be supported by a medical certificate issued by a registered medical practitioner, submitted within 3 working days of resuming duty.
4.4 Leave records shall be maintained by the Establishments Division and reconciled quarterly against the departmental attendance register.

5. Special Provisions

5.1 Employees engaged in essential services may have annual leave restricted during periods of declared public emergency, subject to compensatory leave being granted thereafter.
5.2 Any employee absent without approved leave for more than 3 consecutive working days shall be reported to the Establishments Division for disciplinary review.
